. Electric Potential
Point charges of +1C, -2C, +3C, and -4C are placed at the corners of a square with sides of 1.0 m (in order). Calculate the electric potential at the center of the square.


### 1. Variables and Geometry
* **Charges:** $Q_1 = +1\text{ C}$, $Q_2 = -2\text{ C}$, $Q_3 = +3\text{ C}$, $Q_4 = -4\text{ C}$
* 
* **Distance ($r$):** The distance from any corner to the center of a $1.0\text{ m}$ square is:
* 
    $$r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{ m} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$$
  
* **Constant ($k$):** $\approx 8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$

---

### 2. The Formula
The potential at a point due to multiple charges is the sum of the potentials created by each charge:

$$V_{\text{total}} = k \frac{Q_1}{r} + k \frac{Q_2}{r} + k \frac{Q_3}{r} + k \frac{Q_4}{r}$$

Since the distance $r$ is the same for all corners, we can simplify this by factoring out $k/r$:

$$V_{\text{total}} = \frac{k}{r} (Q_1 + Q_2 + Q_3 + Q_4)$$



---

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation
**Step 1: Sum the charges**

$$\sum Q = (+1) + (-2) + (+3) + (-4) = -2\text{ C}$$

**Step 2: Plug into the simplified equation**

$$V_{\text{total}} = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9}{\sqrt{2}/2} \times (-2)$$

$$V_{\text{total}} = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9}{0.707} \times (-2)$$

**Step 3: Solve**
$$V_{\text{total}} \approx (1.27 \times 10^{10}) \times (-2)$$

$$V_{\text{total}} \approx -2.54 \times 10^{10}\text{ V}$$
