### 1. Variables
* **Corner charge ($Q$):** $1.0\text{ C}$
* **Center charge ($q$):** $-2.0\text{ C}$
* **Side ($s$):** $1.0\text{ m}$
* **Coulomb constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$

---

### 2. Geometry
The distance $r$ from a corner to the center is half the diagonal:
$$r = \frac{s\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{1.0\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{ m}$$

---

### 3. Magnitude of Single Force ($F$)
Using Coulomb's Law, $F = k \frac{|Q q|}{r^2}$:
$$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{|(1.0)(-2.0)|}{(\sqrt{2}/2)^2}$$
$$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{2.0}{0.5}$$
$$F = 3.596 \times 10^{10}\text{ N}$$

---

### 4. Vector Summation


Due to the symmetry of the square, each corner charge pulls the center charge toward itself with an identical magnitude. 

* **Diagonal A:** $\vec{F}_{\text{top-left}}$ and $\vec{F}_{\text{bottom-right}}$ are equal and opposite.
* **Diagonal B:** $\vec{F}_{\text{top-right}}$ and $\vec{F}_{\text{bottom-left}}$ are equal and opposite.

$$\sum \vec{F} = \vec{F}_1 + \vec{F}_2 + \vec{F}_3 + \vec{F}_4 = 0$$

---

### Final Result
* **Net Force Magnitude:** $0\text{ N}$
* **Direction:** N/A (Equilibrium)
