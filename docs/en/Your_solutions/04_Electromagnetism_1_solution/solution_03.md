
### 1. Set up the Variables
* **$q_1 = +4\text{ C}$** (at the origin, $x = 0$)
* **$q_2 = +9\text{ C}$** (at $x = 2\text{ m}$)
* **$q_3 = +1\text{ C}$** (at position $x$)
* **Distance from $q_1$ to $q_3$:** $x$
* **Distance from $q_2$ to $q_3$:** $(2 - x)$



---

### 2. Equality of Forces
For equilibrium, $F_1 = F_2$. Using Coulomb's Law ($k \frac{q_a q_b}{r^2}$):

$$k \frac{q_1 q_3}{x^2} = k \frac{q_2 q_3}{(2-x)^2}$$

**We** can cancel $k$ and $q_3$ from both sides immediately:

$$\frac{q_1}{x^2} = \frac{q_2}{(2-x)^2}$$

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Substitute the charge values**

$$\frac{4}{x^2} = \frac{9}{(2-x)^2}$$

**Step 2: Take the square root of both sides (the cleanest method)**

$$\sqrt{\frac{4}{x^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{9}{(2-x)^2}}$$

$$\frac{2}{x} = \frac{3}{2-x}$$

**Step 3: Cross-multiply and solve for $x$**

$$2(2 - x) = 3x$$

$$4 - 2x = 3x$$

$$4 = 5x$$

$$x = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8\text{ m}$$
