Lorentz Force acting on Wire
A straight wire 2.0 m long carries a current of 10 A. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of B = 0.5 T. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:

a) 
90
∘
 b) 
45
∘
 c) 
0
∘




### 1. Identify the Given Information
* **Current ($I$):** $10\text{ A}$
* **Length ($L$):** $2.0\text{ m}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5\text{ T}$

---

### 2. The Formula
The magnitude of the force ($F$) is given by:

$$F = ILB\sin(\theta)$$
where $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field.



---

### 3. Calculations

**a) $\theta = 90^\circ$ (Perpendicular)**

Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, the force is at its maximum:

$$F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)(1)$$

**$F = 10\text{ N}$**

**b) $\theta = 45^\circ$**

Since $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$:

$$F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)(0.707)$$

**$F \approx 7.07\text{ N}$**

**c) $\theta = 0^\circ$ (Parallel)**

Since $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$:

$$F = (10)(2.0)(0.5)(0)$$

**$F = 0\text{ N}$**
