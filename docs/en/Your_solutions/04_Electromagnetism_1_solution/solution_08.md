### 1. Identify the Given Information
* **Charge ($q$):** $2 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
* **Mass ($m$):** $4 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5\text{ T}$
* **Velocity ($v$):** $10^6\text{ m/s}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (perpendicular)

---

### 2. The Formula
The magnetic component of the Lorentz force is given by:

$$F = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$

In terms of magnitude:

$$F = qvB\sin(\theta)$$

Since the velocity is perpendicular to the field, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$, simplifying the formula to:

$$F = qvB$$





### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation
**Step 1: Plug in the values**

$$F = (2 \times 10^{-19}) \times (10^6) \times (0.5)$$

**Step 2: Simplify the powers of ten**

$$F = (2 \times 0.5) \times (10^{-19} \times 10^6)$$

$$F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}$$

**Step 3: Final Magnitude**

$$F = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\text{ N}$$
