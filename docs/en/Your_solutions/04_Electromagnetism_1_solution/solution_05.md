### 1. The Equilibrium Condition
For the proton to levitate, the magnitude of the electric force ($F_e$) must equal the magnitude of the gravitational force ($F_g$):

$$F_e = F_g$$

**We** define these forces as:

* **Electric Force:** $F_e = qE$ (where $E$ is the electric field strength)
* 
* **Gravitational Force:** $F_g = mg$

### 2. The Formula
By setting them equal, **we** get:

$$qE = mg$$

To find the field strength ($E$), **we** rearrange the equation:

$$E = \frac{mg}{q}$$



### 3. Step-by-Step Calculation
**Step 1: Identify the values**

* $m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
* $g = 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
* $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$

**Step 2: Calculate the weight ($mg$)**

$$F_g = (1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \times 9.8$$
$$F_g \approx 1.6366 \times 10^{-26}\text{ N}$$

**Step 3: Solve for $E$**

$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ N/C}$$
