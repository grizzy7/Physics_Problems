To determine which object has greater momentum, we use the formula for linear momentum:

$$p = m \cdot v$$

where:
* $p$ is momentum
* $m$ is mass
* $v$ is velocity



### 1. Calculate the momentum of the fly
First, we must ensure the units are consistent. We will convert grams to kilograms (the standard SI unit).
* **Mass ($m_1$):** $2\text{ g} = 0.002\text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_1$):** $10\text{ m/s}$

$$p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002\text{ kg} \times 10\text{ m/s} = 0.02\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

### 2. Calculate the momentum of the tennis ball
* **Mass ($m_2$):** $60\text{ g} = 0.06\text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_2$):** $1\text{ m/s}$

$$p_{\text{ball}} = 0.06\text{ kg} \times 1\text{ m/s} = 0.06\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

### 3. Comparison
* Momentum of fly: $0.02\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
* Momentum of tennis ball: $0.06\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$

Since $0.06 > 0.02$, the **60-gram tennis ball** has greater momentum. Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, the tennis ball's much larger mass (30 times greater than the fly) gives it three times the momentum of the fly.
