### 1. The Relationship Between Energy and Height
When a ball is at its maximum height after a bounce, its kinetic energy is zero, and its total mechanical energy is equal to its gravitational potential energy ($PE$). The formula for potential energy is:
$$PE = mgh$$
Where:
* $m$ is the mass of the ball.
* $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity ($9.81\text{ m/s}^2$).
* $h$ is the height.

Since $m$ and $g$ remain constant, the mechanical energy is directly proportional to the height ($E \propto h$). Therefore, if the ball loses 30% of its energy, it will also reach 70% ($0.7$) of its previous height.



### 2. Calculating the Heights
* **Initial Height ($h_0$):** $2.0\text{ m}$
* **Energy Retention Factor:** Since it loses 30%, it retains $100\% - 30\% = 70\%$ (or $0.7$).

**After the First Bounce:**
The height reached after the first bounce ($h_1$) is 70% of the initial height:
$$h_1 = h_0 \times 0.7$$
$$h_1 = 2.0\text{ m} \times 0.7 = 1.4\text{ m}$$

**After the Second Bounce:**
The height reached after the second bounce ($h_2$) is 70% of the height reached after the first bounce:
$$h_2 = h_1 \times 0.7$$
$$h_2 = 1.4\text{ m} \times 0.7 = 0.98\text{ m}$$

$$h_n = h_0 \times (\text{retention factor})^n$$

**Final Answer:**
