To find the acceleration of the 10 kg block, we need to analyze the forces acting on it. Since the 5 kg block is tied to the wall, it remains stationary relative to the ground, while the 10 kg block slides beneath it.

### 1. Identify the Forces
We define:
* $m_1 = 5 \text{ kg}$ (top block)
* $m_2 = 10 \text{ kg}$ (bottom block)
* $F = 45 \text{ N}$ (applied horizontal force)
* $\mu_k = 0.2$ (coefficient of kinetic friction)
* $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (acceleration due to gravity)



### 2. Calculate Friction Forces on the 10 kg Block
The 10 kg block experiences friction from two surfaces: the interface with the 5 kg block and the interface with the floor.

* **Friction from the 5 kg block ($f_{k1}$):**
    The normal force at this surface is the weight of the 5 kg block.
    $$N_1 = m_1 g = 5 \times 9.8 = 49 \text{ N}$$
    $$f_{k1} = \mu_k N_1 = 0.2 \times 49 = 9.8 \text{ N}$$
    Because the 10 kg block moves to the right, this friction force acts on it to the **left**.

* **Friction from the floor ($f_{k2}$):**
    The normal force at the floor is the total weight of both blocks resting on it.
    $$N_2 = (m_1 + m_2)g = (5 + 10) \times 9.8 = 15 \times 9.8 = 147 \text{ N}$$
    $$f_{k2} = \mu_k N_2 = 0.2 \times 147 = 29.4 \text{ N}$$
    This friction force also acts to the **left**.

### 3. Calculate Net Force and Acceleration
Now, we apply Newton's second law ($F_{net} = m_2 a$) to the 10 kg block:

$$F_{net} = F - f_{k1} - f_{k2}$$
$$F_{net} = 45 \text{ N} - 9.8 \text{ N} - 29.4 \text{ N}$$
$$F_{net} = 45 - 39.2 = 5.8 \text{ N}$$

Finally, solve for acceleration ($a$):
$$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m_2}$$
$$a = \frac{5.8 \text{ N}}{10 \text{ kg}} = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**The acceleration of the 10 kg block is $0.58 \text{ m/s}^2$.**

*(Note: if using $g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$, the calculation yields $a = 0.5 \text{ m/s}^2$.)*
