To find the final speed of the cart and the runner, we use the principle of **Conservation of Momentum**.

### 1. Finding the Final Speed
In an isolated system, the total momentum before an event is equal to the total momentum after the event. 

**Given:**
* Mass of the runner ($m_r$): $70 \text{ kg}$
* Initial velocity of the runner ($v_r$): $3 \text{ m/s}$
* Mass of the cart ($m_c$): $140 \text{ kg}$
* Initial velocity of the cart ($v_c$): $0 \text{ m/s}$

**The formula for conservation of momentum is:**
$$m_r v_r + m_c v_c = (m_r + m_c) v_f$$

Substitute the known values:
$$(70 \text{ kg} \cdot 3 \text{ m/s}) + (140 \text{ kg} \cdot 0 \text{ m/s}) = (70 \text{ kg} + 140 \text{ kg}) \cdot v_f$$
$$210 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} = 210 \text{ kg} \cdot v_f$$

Solving for the final velocity ($v_f$):
$$v_f = \frac{210}{210} = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

The final speed of the cart with the runner is **$1 \text{ m/s}$**.

### 2. Is Kinetic Energy Conserved?
To determine if kinetic energy is conserved, we compare the total kinetic energy before and after the runner jumps on the cart.

**Initial Kinetic Energy ($KE_i$):**
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2} m_r v_r^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_c v_c^2$$
$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2} (70 \text{ kg}) (3 \text{ m/s})^2 + 0$$
$$KE_i = 35 \cdot 9 = 315 \text{ J}$$

**Final Kinetic Energy ($KE_f$):**
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2} (m_r + m_c) v_f^2$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2} (210 \text{ kg}) (1 \text{ m/s})^2$$
$$KE_f = 105 \text{ J}$$

### Conclusion
**No, kinetic energy is not conserved.** The initial kinetic energy was $315 \text{ J}$, but the final kinetic energy is only $105 \text{ J}$. This is a **perfectly inelastic collision** because the two objects stick together and move as one after the impact. In such collisions, kinetic energy is always lost as it is transformed into other forms of energy, such as heat, sound, and internal work done to deform the objects or stabilize the runner on the cart.
