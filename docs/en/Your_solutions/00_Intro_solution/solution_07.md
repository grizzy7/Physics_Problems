### Fly and Bicycle Problem

A bicycle is **10 meters** from a wall and moves toward it at **1 m/s**.  
A fly starts from the bicycle and flies toward the wall at **2 m/s**, bouncing back and forth between the bicycle and the wall until the bicycle reaches the wall.

### Step 1: Time for the bicycle to reach the wall

Distance = 10 m  
Speed = 1 m/s

Time = Distance / Speed

Time = 10 / 1  
Time = 10 seconds

### Step 2: Distance the fly travels

The fly flies continuously for the entire **10 seconds** at **2 m/s**.

Distance = Speed × Time

Distance = 2 × 10  
Distance = 20 m

### Final Answer

The total distance the fly travels is:

20 meters
